# Best Practices for Ocean Turbulence Measurements

## Key References

### Comparing Microstructure and Finescale Estimates

- **Whalen, C. B. (2021).** Best practices for comparing ocean turbulence
  measurements across spatiotemporal scales. *J. Atmos. Ocean. Technol.*,
  38(4), 837--841.
  [doi:10.1175/JTECH-D-20-0175.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/JTECH-D-20-0175.1)
  -- [papers/Whalen_2021_Best_Practices_Comparing_Ocean_Turbulence.pdf](papers/Whalen_2021_Best_Practices_Comparing_Ocean_Turbulence.pdf)

  Demonstrates that comparing averages of dissipation rate on different
  spatiotemporal scales can generate spurious discrepancies of up to an
  order of magnitude in regions of strong turbulence, due to the
  near-lognormal distribution of epsilon.  Care must be taken to ensure
  that equivalent quantities are being compared.

### Spectral Quality Assessment

- **Lueck, R. G. (2022).** The statistics of oceanic turbulence
  measurements. Part 1: Shear variance and dissipation rates.
  *J. Atmos. Ocean. Technol.*, 39(10), 1489--1506.
  [doi:10.1175/JTECH-D-21-0051.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/JTECH-D-21-0051.1)

  Introduces the figure of merit (FM) for assessing shear spectral
  quality.  FM < 1.0 indicates the observed spectrum is consistent with
  the Nasmyth model; FM > 1.15 suggests contamination (vibration,
  collision, or other non-turbulent variance).

### Turbulent Diffusivity and Mixing Efficiency

- **Osborn, T. R. (1980).** Estimates of the local rate of vertical
  diffusion from dissipation measurements. *J. Phys. Oceanogr.*,
  10(1), 83--89.
  [doi:10.1175/1520-0485(1980)010<0083:EOTLRO>2.0.CO;2](https://doi.org/10.1175/1520-0485(1980)010%3C0083:EOTLRO%3E2.0.CO;2)

  Foundational paper relating TKE dissipation rate to turbulent
  diffusivity via the mixing efficiency parameter Gamma = 0.2.

- **Osborn, T. R., & Cox, C. S. (1972).** Oceanic fine structure.
  *Geophys. Fluid Dyn.*, 3(1), 321--345.
  [doi:10.1080/03091927208236085](https://doi.org/10.1080/03091927208236085)

  Establishes the relationship between thermal variance dissipation
  rate chi and turbulent thermal diffusivity K_T.

- **Gregg, M. C., D'Asaro, E. A., Riley, J. J., & Kunze, E. (2018).**
  Mixing efficiency in the ocean. *Annu. Rev. Mar. Sci.*, 10,
  443--473.
  [doi:10.1146/annurev-marine-121916-063643](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-marine-121916-063643)

  Comprehensive review of mixing efficiency observations and theory,
  including evidence that Gamma varies with turbulence intensity and
  stratification.

### Shear Probe and Nasmyth Spectrum

- **Nasmyth, P. W. (1970).** *Oceanic turbulence.* Ph.D. thesis,
  University of British Columbia.

  Original measurements of the universal shear spectrum.

- **Oakey, N. S. (1982).** Determination of the rate of dissipation
  of turbulent energy from simultaneous temperature and velocity shear
  microstructure measurements. *J. Phys. Oceanogr.*, 12(3), 256--271.
  [doi:10.1175/1520-0485(1982)012<0256:DOTROD>2.0.CO;2](https://doi.org/10.1175/1520-0485(1982)012%3C0256:DOTROD%3E2.0.CO;2)

### Thermal Variance Dissipation (Chi)

- **Batchelor, G. K. (1959).** Small-scale variation of convected
  quantities like temperature in turbulent fluid. Part 1. General
  discussion and the case of small conductivity. *J. Fluid Mech.*,
  5(1), 113--133.
  [doi:10.1017/S002211205900009X](https://doi.org/10.1017/S002211205900009X)

  Theoretical prediction of the temperature gradient spectrum shape at
  high wavenumbers (the Batchelor spectrum).

- **Kraichnan, R. H. (1968).** Small-scale structure of a scalar field
  convected by turbulence. *Phys. Fluids*, 11(5), 945--953.
  [doi:10.1063/1.1692063](https://doi.org/10.1063/1.1692063)

  Alternative model for the scalar gradient spectrum, differing from
  Batchelor in the form of the high-wavenumber rolloff.

- **Dillon, T. M., & Caldwell, D. R. (1980).** The Batchelor spectrum
  and dissipation in the upper ocean. *J. Geophys. Res.*, 85(C4),
  1910--1916.
  [doi:10.1029/JC085iC04p01910](https://doi.org/10.1029/JC085iC04p01910)

### Coherent Noise Removal

- **Goodman, L., Levine, E. R., & Lueck, R. G. (2006).** On measuring
  the terms of the turbulent kinetic energy budget from an AUV.
  *J. Atmos. Ocean. Technol.*, 23(7), 977--990.
  [doi:10.1175/JTECH1889.1](https://doi.org/10.1175/JTECH1889.1)

  Method for removing coherent vibration contamination from shear
  spectra using accelerometer cross-spectra.
